Our Philosophy

The fundraiser isn’t
the strategy. The budget is.

“The gap between the baseline budget and the dream budget — that’s your campaign goal. Not a guess. A number with a job.”
Most school programs set fundraising goals based on last year’s number, a neighboring program, or a figure that feels ambitious. None of those are a budget.

School Solutions Global reverses the sequence. We start with what the program actually costs, build a picture of what it would cost if it were fully funded, and set the fundraising goal as the gap between those two numbers.

The fundraiser funds the budget. The budget drives the fundraiser. Every dollar has a job before the first ask is ever made.
1
Budget Baseline
What does the program actually cost? District funds, booster funds, all expenses. Mapped clearly, often for the first time.
2
Budget of Their Dreams
What would the program cost if it were fully funded? New uniforms. Full travel budget. Every instrument working. Set the vision.
3
Fundraising to Match
The gap between baseline and dream is the campaign goal. Specific. Defensible. Explainable to students, parents, and donors.
4
Every Dollar Has a Job
Donors give to line items, not a general fund. Uniforms. Travel. Instruments. They see the impact before they give.
